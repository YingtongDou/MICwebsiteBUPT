Call For Papers

International School, Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications
May 10th, BEIJING, CHINA
Model International Conference, 2014
Technology and Humanity


Background
   Technology and humanity are precious treasure which human created in the evolution of human society. People develop technology and humanity and also benefit from the development of technology and humanity. Although they have different meaning and maybe have some contraction, neither of them can be ignored. Attaching the same importance to technology and humanity, people can not only explore the secret of truth, but also reflect on the destiny and value of individuals. They are just like two wings of the society, taking human to a higher level of civilization. Based on the theme importance to technology and humanity, people can not only explore the secretPosts and Telecommunications will hold a model international conference in May 10th, 2014. Related fields of the conference are telecommunication, computer science, E-commerce and internet of things.

Objectives
      It is a good platform for students to communicate and exchange ideas. It provides students with a better understanding of our majors know more about the relationship of technology and humanity, and also create a blueprint of how to use the knowledge in the future.

Topics
The topics include, but are not limited to:
  1. Environmental Protection & Sustainable Development
  2. Security, Privacy & Trust
  3. Engineering, Technology & General Education
  4. Engineering, Technology & Culture
  5. The Cultivation of Interaction Talent
  6. Human-Computer Interaction & Artificial Intelligence
  7. Science and Cultural Inheritance
  8. Harmonization of Science Education and Humanities Education
  9. Technology, Health & Human Life
  10. Social Network Services

Preparation of Papers
      Each participant is required to submit a paper in Contributed Paper Form (Click here to view and download IEEE -Paper Template), and an electronic version of the paper. The submitted paper is required to show in turn the paper title, submit date, affiliation (your class number), author's name, students' number and abstract at the top of the first page.
      In the paper title, capitalize the first letter of all the words, except for some articles (a, an, the), coordinating conjunctions (and, or, for, but, nor) and most short propositions unless they are the first or last words.
      Every paper must contain an Abstract of 150-200 words, which appear in text in boldface type. After the abstract, there must be 3-5 keywords (also be referred to as Index Terms).
      Text Section Headings: There are four levels of section headings with established specifications: primary; secondary; tertiary; and quaternary heads. Enumeration of section heads is desirable, but not required; the choice must be consistent throughout the paper. All the four levels of section headings are enumerated by Arabic numerals(1.1.1.1) and justified left above the text; Quaternary headings are identical to other headings, except only the first letter of the heading is capitalized.
      Reference and Acknowledgment headings are not enumerated. They are simply primary headings without labels.
      Appendix headings are numbered with letters (Appendix A). If there is only one Appendix in the paper, the Appendix heading is unnumbered.
      Every paper must contain an Abstract of 150-200 words, which appear in text in boldface type. After the abstract, there must be 3-5 keywords (also be referred to as Index Terms).
      The paper must be 1,000-1,500 words excluding references and appendices.
